How to cook a Pasta

Ingridients:

  • Spaghetti (or any other long pasta)
  • Pancetta or guanciale (Italian cured pork products)
  • Eggs (usually just the yolks)
  • Parmesan cheese (or Pecorino Romano)
  • Black pepper
  • Garlic
  • Onions
  • Heavy cream
  • Butter

Instructions :

  1. Boil water: Fill a large pot with enough water to cover the amount of pasta you want to cook.
    Bring the water to a boil over high heat.
  2. Add salt: Once the water is boiling, add salt to taste.
    Adding salt to the water enhances the flavor of the pasta.
  3. Add pasta: Add the pasta to the boiling water and stir gently to ensure
    that the pasta does not stick together.
  4. Cook pasta: Cook the pasta according to the package instructions. The cooking time will depend on the type and size of pasta.
    Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. Test for doneness: After a few minutes, begin testing the pasta for doneness by tasting a piece.
    The pasta should be cooked "al dente," which means it should be
    tender but still have a slight bite to it
  6. Drain the pasta: Once the pasta is cooked, use a colander to drain the water.
  7. Serve the pasta: Add the cooked pasta to your desired sauce, or
    serve it plain with olive oil, cheese, or any other toppings of your choice.
  8. Enjoy your pasta: Sit back, relax, and enjoy your delicious pasta dish!
